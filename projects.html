Featured Assignment · Dam Safety

Luti Dam Failure — Forensic Investigation

Led NDSA's rapid forensic investigation of the Luti Dam failure in Chhattisgarh — one of the first post-failure investigations conducted under the Dam Safety Act 2021 framework.

Dam failures in India have historically been inadequately documented from a forensic standpoint. The Luti Dam investigation involved field investigation of the breach site, analysis of failure mechanism and sequence, review of available design and inspection records, and preparation of findings for NDSA. The assignment required combining rapid risk assessment methodology, structural engineering judgment, and regulatory understanding of what DSA 2021 requires post-failure. This type of forensic work is rare — and the institutional knowledge gained is directly applicable to understanding failure modes in other ageing dams across the country.

Specialist Capability
Post-Failure Forensic Investigation

When a dam fails, the immediate questions are: what happened, why, and what does it mean for other similar structures? These questions require a rare combination of structural engineering knowledge, field investigation experience, and regulatory understanding.

The Luti Dam investigation in Chhattisgarh was conducted as one of NDSA's first formal post-failure investigations under the DSA 2021 framework. The methodology — rapid field assessment, breach mechanism analysis, design record review, and findings documentation — is now available as a consulting service for dam owners, state governments, and regulators facing similar situations.

Field breach investigation + design record review + failure mechanism analysis

NDSA findings report — breach cause, failure sequence, regulatory implications
Significance

Transferable Value
Failure modes identified inform risk screening of structurally similar dams
