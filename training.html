The Training Philosophy

Not Lectures — Actionable Knowledge

DSA 2021 has created obligations that most state dam safety officials are not yet equipped to fulfil. The gap is not awareness — most officials know the Act exists. The gap is operational: how do you actually conduct a pre-monsoon inspection? How do you fill the DHARMA questionnaire? How do you interpret a rapid risk assessment score? What does an EAP actually need to contain? These programmes answer those questions directly, with reference to real cases and actual regulatory forms.

⊙
Current & Regulatory

All content is aligned with DSA 2021, NDSA Regulations 2024, and the latest NDSA/CWC guidelines — not older frameworks. Participants leave knowing what applies today, not what applied in 2015.

◎
Field-Referenced

Drawing on 35+ years of CWC and NDSA field experience — including World Bank in-situ training at Ichari Dam and the Luti Dam forensic investigation. Case examples are real, not hypothetical.

⊕
Audience-Calibrated

Programmes designed for the actual audience — dam safety officials who need to apply the law, not researchers who need to understand it theoretically. Language, examples, and depth adjusted accordingly.

◈
Modular & Flexible

Modules can be delivered standalone or combined into 1–3 day programmes. Mix and match based on your state's priority gaps — inspections, risk assessment, EAP, DHARMA, or a comprehensive DSA 2021 overview.

Training Modules

What We Deliver

M1
Foundation
DSA 2021 — The Act, Rules & Regulations Explained

A practical walkthrough of what DSA 2021 actually requires — for dam owners, SDSOs, and state officials. Not a legal lecture; a compliance guide.

  • Specified dam definition — Section 2(x), all five criteria
  • Institutional framework — NDSA, SCDS, SDSO, Dam Safety Unit
  • Obligations of dam owners — Sections 12–15, 26, 30
  • Penalties and enforcement — Chapter VI
  • NDSA Regulations 2024 — what's new
Half day · All audiences
M2
Inspection
Dam Inspection Methodology

What to look for, how to record it, and how to use the DHARMA inspection questionnaire. Practical guidance for pre-monsoon and post-monsoon inspections.

  • NDSA Circular 1/2022 — deadlines and requirements
  • Visual inspection — what to observe and how to grade
  • Structural, mechanical, and instrumentation checks
  • DHARMA questionnaire — field walkthrough
  • Distress condition identification and reporting
Full day · Dam safety officials, DSOs
M3
Risk Assessment
Rapid Risk Assessment — RRSSD Methodology

World Bank DRIP methodology for Tier 1 rapid risk screening — how to compute FI × PI, interpret sub-scores, and identify which failure modes need deeper assessment.

  • FI = f(TC, EC, SP) — sub-score structure and computation
  • PI — Population Impact scoring
  • Risk Index computation and what it means
  • Sub-score interpretation — which failure mode is dominant
  • Tier 2 referral — when and why
Full day · Dam safety engineers, DSOs
M4
Emergency Preparedness
EAP Preparation — NDSA Template Walkthrough

Practical preparation of Emergency Action Plans — hazard classification, dam break analysis inputs, inundation mapping, and the NDSA-approved EAP template.

  • NDSA Hazard Classification Regulations 2024 — PCI and H1–H6
  • Dam break analysis — breach parameters and what they mean
  • Inundation mapping — how to read and use the outputs
  • EAP template — section by section walkthrough
  • Mock drill planning and community awareness
Full day · Dam owners, SDSO, DDMA officials
M5
Digital Compliance
DHARMA Portal — Operations & Compliance

Hands-on training on NDSA's DHARMA digital platform — registration, inspection data entry, report upload, and compliance tracking. Delivered at NWA Pune for NDSA (April 2024).

  • Dam registration and basic data entry
  • Inspection questionnaire — field to portal
  • Report upload — CDSE, inspection, EAP
  • Hazard classification and RA results entry
  • Compliance status tracking and NDSA visibility
Half to full day · All DHARMA users
M6
Construction Safety
First Filling & Construction-Stage Safety under DSA 2021

The critical bridge between construction completion and operational safety — what DSA 2021 requires before first filling, during filling, and how construction-stage EAPs differ from operational EAPs.

  • Construction-stage vs operational EAP — key differences
  • Pre-filling SoP — checklist and sign-off requirements
  • Monitoring during first filling — instrumentation response
  • When does DSA 2021 kick in? — new vs existing dams
  • Case study — monsoon arrival before crest completion
Half day · Dam owners, construction engineers, SDSOs
M7
Hydrology
Hydrological Safety Review — PMF, SPF & IDF Adequacy

Understanding and reviewing the design flood for existing dams — when is the IDF inadequate, what does a PMF review involve, and how does NDSA approach IDF deficiencies.

  • IDF standards — PMF, SPF, return period floods
  • IS 11223 classification vs DSA 2021 requirements
  • GLOF — what it is and when it matters
  • Identifying IDF deficiency — signs and triggers
  • ALARP framework for remediation planning
Full day · Dam safety engineers, design engineers
M8
Comprehensive
DSA 2021 Comprehensive Programme

A full 2–3 day programme combining M1 through M5 for new state dam safety organisations or states commencing DSA 2021 implementation from scratch. Delivered in HP, Uttarakhand, and Manipur.

  • Day 1: Act, Rules, Regulations, institutional setup
  • Day 2: Inspections, instrumentation, risk assessment
  • Day 3: EAP, DHARMA, compliance obligations
  • Case studies from the trainer's NDSA experience
  • Practical exercises using real NDSA formats
2–3 days · Mixed state audience

How Programmes
Are Structured

Programmes are adapted to the commissioning organisation's priorities, audience composition, and available time. All content is current as of DSA 2021 and NDSA Regulations 2024.

Audience
Dam safety officials, Dam Safety Officers, SDSO staff, dam owners' technical representatives, design engineers, DDMA officials — or mixed audiences combining multiple roles.
Duration
Half day (single module), full day (2–3 modules), or 2–3 day comprehensive programme. Online or in-person. State-hosted or neutral venue.
Materials
Presentation slides, module workbooks, NDSA format templates (EAP, inspection checklist, DHARMA questionnaire), and reference documents — all provided to participants.
Language
English with Hindi explanation where needed. Key regulatory terms and NDSA formats provided in both languages.
Certification
Participation certificates issued. For NDSA-commissioned programmes, certificates carry NDSA authority. For state programmes, certificates issued under the commissioning state government.
